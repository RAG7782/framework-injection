## 1. The Research Program: Vision and Architecture

The Artisanal Intelligence Program (AIP) is a Lakatosian research program that proposes a fundamental reorientation of human-AI interaction. Its central thesis is that the quality of AI output is not proportional to the quality of the command issued, but to the quality of the reasoning method transferred. This single insight -- that AI should be educated rather than commanded or merely informed -- organizes an entire ecosystem of interconnected projects spanning philosophy, linguistics, cognitive science, information retrieval, memory systems, multi-agent coordination, and operational tooling.

The program was created by Renato Aparecido Gomes, a Brazilian corporate and tax attorney with over twenty years of legal practice, holder of an MBA in Applied Artificial Intelligence from FMA/Pareto, one of twenty-five ambassadors of the TESS Creative Partners program, and president of the Instituto Luiz Gama (a human rights organization focused on racial equality). Gomes's background is significant because the research program itself embodies cross-domain bridge thinking: it connects 2,500 years of philosophical tradition (Plato, Aristotle, Wittgenstein, Peirce) with contemporary LLM engineering, applies biological models to software agent coordination, and draws on linguistic typology to explain why certain interactions with AI succeed while others fail.

The program is structured as a five-level abstraction hierarchy. At Level 5 sits the research program itself (AIP). At Level 4, the epistemic praxis: Artesanato Digital (Digital Craftsmanship). At Level 3, the mechanisms: Framework Injection, Adversarial Tempering Protocol (ATP), Linguagem Plena (Full Language), and Semiotic Density Engineering. At Level 2, the systems: AGORA-OS, IMI, STEER, SYMBIONT, and NeuroAgent. At Level 1, the artifacts: papers, code, skills, hooks, benchmarks, and MCP servers.

The Lakatosian structure is explicit. The hard core holds three non-negotiable commitments: intelligence is craft, human expertise is semantically transferable to AI, and governance must be constitutional. The protective belt consists of specific taxonomies, metrics, and implementations that can be revised without threatening the core. The positive heuristic directs extension to new domains, formalization of connections, and empirical validation. The negative heuristic forbids reduction to prompt engineering and forbids abandoning ethical primacy.

As of April 2026, the program has produced six Zenodo DOIs [Decay: frozen — historical] (STEER: 10.5281/zenodo.19325724; IMI Core: 10.5281/zenodo.19325745; SYMBIONT: 10.5281/zenodo.19325749; IMI Theory: 10.5281/zenodo.19341740; Founding Paper AD+FI: 10.5281/zenodo.19344789; Semiotic Density: 10.5281/zenodo.19382350), three SSRN submissions, one ANTS 2026 submission (International Conference on Swarm Intelligence, Darmstadt, Germany), five public GitHub repositories under Apache 2.0 license, one PyPI package (steer-retrieval v0.1.0), and an arXiv submission pending endorsement [Decay: fast — transient state]. The academic outreach has yielded positive responses from researchers at NYU (Ravfogel) and other institutions.

## 2. Artesanato Digital: The Philosophical Foundation

Artesanato Digital (Digital Craftsmanship) is formally classified as an epistemic praxis in the Aristotelian-Freirean sense: theory-informed, ethically-grounded, reflective practice for the creation, transfer, and governance of artificial intelligence. The term "praxis" is used precisely -- not mere practice (techne), but practice informed by theory (episteme) and oriented toward ethical ends (phronesis). Freire's reformulation deepens this: praxis as the dialectical unity of reflection and action.

The concept originated circa 2024 under the name "Artesanato Algoritmico" and was renamed for accessibility. Its philosophical arc spans from Plato's evocative language (the Cratylus and Theaetetus -- language as bridge between intention and interpretation), through Aristotle's logical structures (the Organon, Rhetoric, and Poetics -- topoi as reasoning templates), to medieval grammar (Panini, Sibawayh, Dionisio Thrax -- language as normative system). The founding paper (Paper 8, "From Commands to Cognition," DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19344789) traces this 2,500-year arc explicitly and argues that prompt engineering recapitulates the history of language but stops at the grammar stage, while Digital Craftsmanship completes the arc.

### The Three Dogmas

Digital Craftsmanship rests on three non-negotiable principles:

**Dogma 1: Interaction as Speech Act with Full Language.** Drawing on Austin's speech act theory and Searle's taxonomy of illocutionary forces, every interaction with an AI agent is treated as a speech act. The critical insight is that Framework Injection constitutes a declarative speech act -- it does not instruct the model but constitutes a new reasoning reality. The opposite of Full Language is "digital pidgin" {Reified: complaint about ChatGPT → formal linguistic diagnosis, Paper 8, Mar/2026} -- an impoverished command register that the program estimates exploits less than thirty percent of LLM capabilities [Hypothesis: no empirical validation], analogous to how contact pidgins sacrifice depth for immediate utility.

**Dogma 2: Form Follows Function.** The structure of the interaction artifact must be derived from its purpose -- the domain, the task, the quality requirements -- not from generic templates. This principle is operationalized through the Adaptive FI discovery (discussed in the Framework Injection section), which demonstrated empirically that one-size-fits-all frameworks harm frontier models [Validated: FI benchmark, 3 real-world cases | Triangulation: 1].

**Dogma 3: Adversarial Tempering.** Every high-stakes output must undergo adversarial validation. The Adversarial Tempering Protocol (ATP), formerly called CAAJ 2.0 {Reified: renamed with metallurgical metaphor, Mar/2026}, is a four-phase validation mechanism inspired by metallurgical tempering: Forge (initial generation under framework), Strike (adversarial stress testing), Anneal (integration of corrections), and Quench (final hardening with quality certification). Untempered output is draft output; only tempered output is deliverable.

### The Seven Operational Layers

Digital Craftsmanship organizes practice into seven layers, from most abstract to most concrete: L7 Epistemological (what counts as knowledge and valid reasoning), L6 Semiotic (sign design for algorithmic interpretation), L5 Pragmatic (speech act design and illocutionary force selection), L4 Architectural (framework design and domain decomposition), L3 Procedural (workflow design and iteration protocols), L2 Technical (token management and model-specific adaptations), and L1 Operational (deployment, monitoring, quality assurance).

The twelve essential elements span domain analysis, expert knowledge elicitation, framework architecture, sign engineering, speech act design, scaffolding calibration, load management, adversarial tempering, ethical governance, quality assurance, knowledge management, and reflective practice. These are organized into a 13-step standard procedure across five phases: Analysis (steps 1-3), Design (steps 4-6), Implementation (steps 7-9), Validation (steps 10-12), and Governance (step 13).

The primary paper treatment of Artesanato Digital is Paper 6 ("Digital Craftsmanship: A Semiotic Framework...") at `/experimentos/papers/paper6-artesanato-digital/paper.tex`, which is subsumed by the founding Paper 8. Source materials include the original philosophical PDF, the AGORA-OS whitepapers (v4.0, v5.0), and the 13-step standard procedure.

## 3. Framework Injection: The Core Mechanism

Framework Injection (FI) is defined as the epistemic mechanism of unidirectional transfer of complete domain-specific reasoning methods from human expert to AI agent, encoded in natural language, operating as persistent cognitive substrate within the agent's processing context. Every element of this definition is load-bearing: it is a mechanism (not a technique), the transfer is unidirectional (human to agent), what is transferred is complete reasoning methods (not fragments), encoding is in natural language (not code or fine-tuning), and the framework persists as cognitive substrate (not one-time instruction).

### The Three-Paradigm Taxonomy

The program identifies three eras of human-AI interaction: Command (Prompt Engineering, 2022-2023), Inform (Context Engineering, 2024-2025), and Educate (Framework Injection, 2026) {Reified: practice of teaching AI → formal paradigm, Paper 7, Mar/2026}. These are related by strict inclusion: PE is a subset of CE is a subset of FI [Decay: stable — conceptual]. The metaphorical progression is: giving a recipe (PE), equipping a kitchen (CE), teaching to cook (FI). The critical distinction is epistemic: PE prescribes actions, CE provides information, FI transfers reasoning methods. The paper articulates this through Peirce's semiotic theory: the prompt is a complex sign with Firstness (syntactic structure), Secondness (explicit and implicit referents), and Thirdness (the interpretant generated in the LLM). The concept of "projected semiosis" {Reified: interpreter-centric sign design → Peircean formalization, Paper 8, Mar/2026} is introduced: designing signs not for what the sender wishes to express but for how the interpretant will be generated -- an interpreter-centric rather than sender-centric approach [Velocity: →].

### The Five-Type Framework Taxonomy

Frameworks are classified into five types: Declarative (what to know -- knowledge hierarchies, source rankings), Procedural (how to reason -- step-by-step methods, decision trees), Evaluative (how to judge -- quality criteria, scoring rubrics), Ethical (what to refuse -- fences, compliance, hard constraints), and Compositional (how to combine -- multi-step workflows, CAAJ/ATP cycles). A critical sixth type was discovered through empirical benchmarking: Meta-cognitive (how to monitor own reasoning), which is specifically designed for frontier models.

### Six (Now Seven) Theoretical Foundations

The original six theoretical foundations are: Semiotics (Peirce, 1931 -- prompt as sign, LLM as algorithmic interpretant, projected semiosis), Linguistic Universals (Greenberg, 1963 -- Agent/Action/Patient and Topic-Comment as prompt primitives), Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky, 1978 -- FI as scaffolding enabling LLM to perform beyond baseline), Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988 -- FI reduces extraneous load by providing schemas), Language Games (Wittgenstein, 1953 -- each domain framework defines valid reasoning moves), and Tacit Knowledge (Polanyi, 1966 -- FI externalizes expert tacit knowledge into transferable format). The seventh foundation, Semiotic Density, is treated in its own section below.

### Adaptive FI: The Most Important Empirical Finding

A rigorous benchmark using real-world cases from major press (Ponzi fraud, MODY misdiagnosis, Brazil tax reform) across two model tiers (Haiku and Sonnet) produced the most significant empirical finding of the program:

| Condition | Haiku | Sonnet |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| PE | 25.00 | 33.00 |
| CE | 23.67 | 31.00 |
| FI-scaffold | 27.33 | 29.33 |
| FI-meta | 24.33 | 32.00 |

Five rules emerged from this data. First, smaller models benefit from FI-scaffold (+9.3% over PE on Haiku) [Validated: benchmark, 3 cases | Triangulation: 1]. Second, frontier models may resist scaffold FI (-11.1% on Sonnet) [Validated: benchmark, 3 cases | Triangulation: 1] because scaffolding becomes a ceiling when the model already knows how to reason. Third, FI-metacognitive partially recovers performance on frontier models (-3% vs -11.1%). Fourth, CE performs worse than PE in all scenarios [Validated: benchmark, 3 cases | Triangulation: 1] -- a finding consistent with Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory [Depth: 1 — read directly] (context without organizational framework equals extraneous cognitive load). Fifth, one-size-fits-all FI is wrong; framework mode must adapt to model capability [Velocity: ⚡].

The solution is two modes: Scaffold mode (all 5 types, explicit, detailed) for smaller models, and Metacognitive mode (evaluative standards 30%, ethical fences 30%, meta-cognitive checkpoints 40%) for frontier models. The metaphors are Vygotskian scaffolding (lifts smaller models above baseline), ceiling (constrains frontier models below baseline), and compass (guides frontier models without constraining).

A particularly striking finding was the FI-metacognitive recovery in the Brazilian tax case: from 21 (scaffold) to 29 (metacognitive), a +38% improvement. This confirmed that scaffold frameworks conflict with frontier models' trained knowledge in domains where the model is already strong, while metacognitive frameworks guide without conflicting.

The benchmark code is at `RAG7782/fi-benchmark` on GitHub, with the adaptive generator (`fi_generator_v2.py`) supporting scaffold, metacognitive, and auto modes.

### Differentiation from Related Work

The five closest related works are: LLM-ACTR (Wu/Oltramari, CMU 2025 -- transfers from computational model, not human expertise; proximity 6/10), DSPy (Stanford, Khattab 2023 -- optimizes pipeline mechanics, not reasoning transfer; 4/10), Context Engineering (Anthropic 2025 -- manages what model sees, not how it thinks; 4/10), Cognitive Design Patterns (AGI 2025 -- describes patterns, doesn't prescribe injection; 3/10), and Meta-Prompting/CoT (2024-2025 -- generic reasoning structure, not domain-specific content; 3/10).

Paper 7 ("Framework Injection: Teaching AI How to Think...") at `/experimentos/papers/paper7-framework-injection/paper.tex` provides the detailed differentiation, but both Papers 6 and 7 are subsumed by the founding Paper 8.

## 4. STEER: Algebraic Operations on Embedding Space

STEER (Semantic Transformation for Embedding-space Exploration in Retrieval), originally called A2RAG {Reified: naming change A2RAG → STEER, Mar/2026}, is the most empirically mature project in the research program. Its central question is deceptively simple: what happens when you algebraically transform a query embedding before retrieval? The formal framework is: R(q, D) = argmax_{d in D} sim(T(q), d), where standard RAG is the identity case T = id.

The project investigated seventeen candidate operations across six sentence transformer architectures and four BEIR benchmarks (2,677 queries), producing 146 result files across 30 experiments [Validated: 30 experiments, 6 models, 5 BEIR datasets | Triangulation: 3]. Of the original seventeen operations, eleven were validated as effective. The paper (v9, 27 pages) reports three headline findings.

**Finding 1: Embedding rotation provides controllable cross-domain retrieval.** Simple normalized linear interpolation toward a target domain replaces results at controlled rates. At alpha=0.1, it replaces 1 of 10 results at -2.2% nDCG cost. This was validated on cross-domain scenarios including drug repurposing across 4 diseases and legal analogy across 4 branches of law.

**Finding 2: Isotropy is the hidden variable.** Low-isotropy models (MiniLM, mean cosine approximately 0.13) benefit from algebraic manipulation, while high-isotropy models (E5, approximately 0.81) are harmed. This finding generalizes beyond STEER to any application manipulating sentence embeddings -- steering vectors, concept erasure, representation engineering.

**Finding 3: Only Euclidean operations on normalized embeddings work.** Hyperbolic, optimal transport, and conceptor-based approaches either reduced to identity, added complexity without benefit, or failed catastrophically. This is reported as a negative result with full rigor.

The mature STEER system composes three techniques -- isotropy correction, adaptive alpha, and multi-vector fusion -- to eliminate nDCG degradation observed with naive rotation. Extended to sixteen operations, the most notable are: contrastive steer (+3.5% nDCG), rotate-away as bias correction (+3.6%), and per-query LLM targets. An automatic operation router achieves F1=0.91 on biomedical benchmarks [Validated: 30 experiments, 6 models, 5 BEIR datasets | Triangulation: 3].

STEER is published as a pip package (`steer-retrieval` v0.1.0 on PyPI), with code at `/Users/renatoaparegomes/a2rag/` and GitHub repository `RAG7782/steer`. The Zenodo DOI is 10.5281/zenodo.19325724, and SSRN abstract ID is 6493121. The arXiv submission targets cs.AI (primary) with cs.IR and cs.CL cross-listings. Conference targets include EMNLP, CIKM, and ECIR.

The connection to the broader research program is direct: STEER provides the quantitative machinery for Semiotic Density Engineering. What FI does qualitatively (composing terms with high semantic density), STEER does quantitatively in embedding space. The rotate_toward operation navigates to adjacent density fields; the subtract_orthogonal operation removes unwanted density dimensions.

## 5. IMI: Cognitive Memory for AI Agents

IMI (Integrated Memory Intelligence) addresses a fundamental limitation of current AI agents: they forget everything between sessions. Its tagline -- "RAG finds what's similar. IMI finds what matters" -- captures the essential distinction. Where RAG treats memory as a searchable database, IMI treats memory as a navigable, multi-resolution space -- an "Infinite Memory Image."

### Core Architecture

IMI v0.2.0 is implemented in approximately 1,200 lines of Python across 12 modules, with 84 passing tests. The architecture introduces three capabilities absent from existing memory systems:

**Multi-resolution zoom.** The agent can view all 500 memories in approximately 5,000 tokens at orbital resolution or zoom into any 3 memories at full reconstructive detail, achieving 50x coverage improvement over RAG at half the token cost. Forgetting is blur, not deletion -- unused memories gradually lose resolution rather than being discarded.

**Affordance-based retrieval.** Memories are indexed by what they enable the agent to do, not by what they contain. This shifts retrieval from semantic similarity to action potential. When an agent encodes "Redis sentinel failover during network partition, 30s of cache misses," IMI extracts affordances like "implement circuit breaker" and "add sentinel monitoring."

**Complementary Learning Systems (CLS).** Dual episodic/semantic stores with consolidation via "dreaming" enable the agent to extract general rules from specific experiences. This is grounded in the neuroscience of memory consolidation (Tononi & Cirelli's Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis) [Depth: 1 — read directly].

Additionally, IMI implements a 3-layer anti-confabulation model distinguishing verified facts from contextual reconstructions from speculative hypotheses. Each memory node carries affect (salience and valence), mass (gravitational pull that resists forgetting), and graph edges (causal links to other memories).

### Key Results

In head-to-head comparison with RAG: tie on static knowledge Q&A (R@5=1.000 each), IMI wins on temporal agent memory (+6.7% domain precision), IMI wins decisively on multi-hop causal chains (100% vs 75% recall) [Validated: benchmark | Triangulation: 1], IMI wins on action retrieval (returns actions vs documents), IMI wins on result freshness (avg 16.8 vs 41.2 days old), but RAG wins slightly on pure retrieval accuracy (-3.7% trade-off for IMI). The bottom line: for agents that just answer questions, use RAG; for agents that need to learn from experience and act on it, use IMI.

### Integrations

IMI ships with six integration paths: MCP Server (6 tools: encode, navigate, dream, search_actions, stats, graph_link), REST API (FastAPI, 7 endpoints), LangChain (IMIMemory class), AMBench CLI for benchmarking, Claude Code skill, and Gradio demo. The AGORA-OS integration bridges KB (semantic knowledge) with IMI (episodic memory) through NAO-to-MemoryNode synchronization.

Advanced features include multi-agent sharing with trust gradient, an SRE memory pack with 500 pre-encoded incidents, and an AMBench leaderboard.

The theoretical foundations are explored in a companion paper (IMI Theory, DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19341740), which develops the "memory as field" metaphor: memories occupy positions in a continuous space organized by semantic proximity, retrieval is rendering rather than search (generating contextually appropriate reconstructions from compressed seeds), and the space has gravitational properties (high-mass memories attract related experiences).

Code lives at `/Users/renatoaparegomes/experimentos/imi/`, GitHub repository `RAG7782/imi`. IMI Core has Zenodo DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19325745 and SSRN abstract ID 6493298.

## 6. SYMBIONT: Bio-Inspired Multi-Agent Coordination

SYMBIONT (Symbiotic Multi-pattern Bio-intelligent Organism for Networked Tasks) attacks the problem that current multi-agent LLM systems rely on static, top-down orchestration that fails when task demands shift dynamically. Its central insight is that biological superorganisms solved this problem through distinct coordination mechanisms producing adaptive collective intelligence without central control.

### The Eight Biological Patterns

SYMBIONT integrates eight biological systems into a unified organism (Bauplan):

1. **Mycorrhizal Fungus Networks (Mycelium):** Adaptive message routing between agents. Just as fungal networks redistribute nutrients across forest ecosystems, the Mycelium routes messages based on agent capability and load.

2. **Slime Mold (Physarum) Topology Engine:** Network self-optimization. The Physarum polycephalum famously replicates optimal transport networks; SYMBIONT uses this for topology optimization of agent communication pathways.

3. **Leaf-cutter Ant (Atta) Caste Registry:** Agent polymorphism with five castes mapped to LLM model tiers -- Minima (Haiku, cost 0.1x) for high-cardinality context work, Media (Sonnet, 1x) for core execution, Major (Opus, 5x) for architecture and tiebreaking, Scout (Haiku, 0.2x) for exploration, and Queen (Opus, 3x) for lifecycle management.

4. **Honeybee (Apis mellifera) Waggle Protocol:** Collective decision-making through quality-weighted voting, analogous to how bee scouts communicate foraging site quality through dance.

5. **Termite (Macrotermes) Mound:** Stigmergy (communication through artifacts) and homeostasis. The Mound monitors system health and triggers automatic responses -- high errors spawn oversight, high latency triggers topology rebalancing.

6. **Starling (Sturnus) Murmuration Bus:** Real-time reflexes at O(log N), where each agent follows only 6-7 neighbors for coherent collective behavior without global coordination.

7. **Wolf + Naked Mole-rat Governor:** Contextual leadership that rotates based on task phase, not hierarchy. Different castes lead in different phases of the five-phase lifecycle: Exploration, Decision, Execution, Validation, Delivery.

8. **Dolphin (Tursiops) Pod Dynamics:** Ephemeral coalitions that form and dissolve based on task demands, similar to how dolphin pods restructure for hunting, play, or defense.

The organism operates under nine emergent laws, the most distinctive being: no agent knows the global plan, the network is smarter than any node, artifacts are communication (stigmergy), leadership is contextual not hierarchical, and reserve is strategy not waste.

### Key Design Principle

The design principle is "functional inspiration, not structural replication" -- each biological mechanism is imported only if the computational problem it solves exists in LLM agent orchestration. SYMBIONT differs from prior bio-inspired multi-agent work (ACO, PSO, ABC) in two respects: it integrates eight patterns into a unified organism rather than applying a single pattern, and it applies biological coordination to LLM agents that reason in natural language -- a qualitatively different substrate where "fitness" is semantic quality rather than a scalar objective.

### Integration and Status

SYMBIONT is implemented in approximately 3,700 lines of Python with 21 modules and 40+ tests. The code lives at `/Users/renatoaparegomes/symbiont/`, with GitHub repository `RAG7782/symbiont`. Zenodo DOI is 10.5281/zenodo.19325749, SSRN abstract ID is 6493319. The ANTS 2026 submission (Late Breaking Results track, 2-page extended abstract) was submitted on March 30, 2026 [Decay: frozen after Jun 2026], to the International Conference on Swarm Intelligence in Darmstadt, Germany (June 8-10, 2026).

An immune-system-inspired governance middleware was developed alongside SYMBIONT, implemented as four MCP servers: immune-guardrails (adaptive invariant enforcement, loop detection, budget tracking, agent suppression), immune-resolution (incident lifecycle from detection through antibody generation to convalescence), immune-scoring (cumulative multi-agent consensus requiring collective thresholds), and immune-tolerance (trust zone management for external integrations with automatic promotion/demotion) [Velocity: →].

## 7. AGORA-OS: The Semantic Operating System

AGORA-OS is the operational platform where the theoretical constructs of Artesanato Digital and Framework Injection are concretized. Originally conceived as a semantic operating system through extensive whitepapers (18,000+ lines), it was evaluated as "concept 8.5/10, execution 3/10" [Depth: 0 — primary self-assessment] -- rich in theory but practically unimplemented. Version 2.0, located at `/Users/renatoaparegomes/experimentos/agora-os-plugin/`, translates the theoretical architecture into operational Claude Code artifacts.

## 8. Semiotic Density: The Seventh Foundation

The Semiotic Density Principle represents what may be the deepest theoretical contribution of the research program, as it explains at a mechanistic level why Framework Injection works.

### The Origin Insight

The discovery came from a deceptively simple example: asking an AI model to generate "a selfie of a giraffe." No instruction specifies camera angle, framing, expression, savanna backdrop, or the comic tension of a non-human animal performing a human act. Yet the model produces all of it. Two words -- "selfie" and "giraffe" -- carry more implicit semantic information than fifty words of explicit instruction could encode. "Selfie" carries an implicit payload: camera angle (arm's length), framing (face-centered), expression (smiling), context (casual). "Giraffe" carries: environment (savanna), scale (tall), behavior (curious). The LLM blends these frames, producing emergent properties without any explicit instruction.

This reveals that FI works not by explicit instruction but by composition of semantic densities. The formal definition: semiotic density {Reified: intuition about FI term weight → Fillmore formalization, Paper 9, Mar/2026} is the ratio of implicit semantic field activated by a term to its explicit surface form [Velocity: ⚡].

### Theoretical Grounding

The principle is grounded in six established theories: Frame Semantics (Fillmore, 1982 -- words activate knowledge structures with roles, relations, and defaults) [Depth: 1 — read directly], Prototype Theory (Rosch, 1975 -- categories have central members that LLMs activate) [Depth: 2 — cited via secondary], Conceptual Blending (Fauconnier & Turner, 2002 -- combining frames produces emergent properties) [Depth: 2 — cited via secondary], Conceptual Metaphors (Lakoff & Johnson, 1980 -- metaphors as structures of thought), Distributional Semantics (Firth, 1957 -- "know a word by the company it keeps," embeddings as compressed frames) [Depth: 1 — read directly], and Spreading Activation (Collins & Loftus, 1975 -- activating one concept irradiates to related ones) [Depth: 1 — read directly].

### The Ideographic Connection

Paper 9 ("Semiotic Density: From Ideographic Writing to Framework Injection," DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19382350) draws an original connection between Chinese ideographic composition, Chain of Density prompting (Adams et al., 2023), and Framework Injection. The central claim: Chinese characters ARE Framework Injection in miniature -- high-density signs carrying frames, prototypes, metaphors, and blends in a single visual unit. The character "ming" (bright) composed of "ri" (sun) + "yue" (moon) is literally a Fauconnier & Turner conceptual blend encoded 3,000 years ago. The character "xin" (trust) composed of "ren" (person) + "yan" (word) encodes an entire social theory of trust.

Three systems are unified under one principle: ideographic characters (radical juxtaposition, 3,000 years), Framework Injection (5-type combination, 2024-2026), and Chain of Density (iterative entity substitution, 2023). All three maximize implicit activation per unit of explicit form through structured composition.

### Semiotic Density Engineering

Five operations for manipulating density are defined, applicable to all five framework types: Densify (replace generic terms with high-density domain terms: "analise" becomes "realize due diligence"), Rarefy (replace overloaded terms with neutral ones when payload misleads: "ataque" becomes "avalie adversarialmente"), Rotate (use STEER to navigate between density fields), Subtract (use STEER to remove unwanted density dimensions), and Blend (create deliberate frame blends: "cirurgiao fiscal" = surgical precision + tax domain).

The key insight connecting to the broader program: "FI works because it operates with terms of HIGH semiotic density whose implicit fields carry domain reasoning in compressed form. PE uses LOW density terms (pidgin). CE provides explicit information but doesn't manage the density of the terms that describe it." Pidgin equals low density; Full Language equals high density.

Framework key terms function as semantic radicals -- just as the Chinese radical for water activates the liquid-field in all characters containing it, "compliance" in a framework activates the regulation-field in all subsequent interactions. New principles derived include Uniformity of Density (from the UID hypothesis: frameworks should distribute density evenly) and Preserve Integrity (from MIT 2025 tokenization research: do not fragment dense terms) [Decay: stable — conceptual].

## 9. Delivery Architecture: The Investigation

The Delivery Architecture (DA) investigation, conducted in April 2026 at `/Users/renatoaparegomes/experimentos/da-experiments/`, represents an active research front exploring a hypothesis that output quality depends on two independent dimensions: reasoning (how the model thinks, addressed by FI) and delivery (how the model organizes the output, addressed by DA). If confirmed, this would mean that FI and DA are orthogonal layers with multiplicative rather than additive effects.

### Tests Executed

Eight tests were run in the first phase:

**T1/T1b: Degradation Curves.** Quality degradation over output length was measured on both Sonnet (showing a plateau after initial drop) and Opus at 64k tokens (showing a U-shaped pattern with templates containing placeholders -- a novel observation about how models manage very long outputs).

**T2: Density Ratio.** FI-generated outputs showed higher semiotic density than PE or CE outputs: +5.1% Type-Token Ratio and -23% repetition rate.

**T3: Stylometry.** FI outputs are structurally different from PE/CE outputs -- more assertive, zero hedging language. This suggests FI does not merely improve content but changes the discourse register itself.

**T4: Blueprint Resistance.** 100% compliance was observed: models follow structural blueprints when provided. This is a prerequisite for DA to work.

**T5: PT vs EN Density.** English outputs were more dense (-14.9%), refuting the initial hypothesis that Portuguese would be denser.

**T6: First Token Commitment.** Confirmed that the opening of an output determines its full structure -- the first token commits the model to a discourse pattern.

**T7: Artisan's Paradox DA.** The strongest finding: +122% improvement when comparing human-crafted blueprints against auto-generated ones {Reified: benchmark anomaly → named phenomenon, DA investigation, Mar/2026} [Validated: DA investigation, 10 tests | Triangulation: 1]. This "Artisan's Paradox" -- that human expertise in crafting output structures cannot be automated away -- is consistent with the broader Framework Injection finding that auto-generated frameworks underperform human-crafted ones.

### Protocol v2

Based on critical feedback (including from Gemini analysis), Protocol v2 was designed with seven experimental arms crossing reasoning (PE/CE/FI) with delivery (free/DA/DA+ATP), nine evaluation criteria including "semantic control," three task classes (informational, analytical, decisional), and controls including same model, temperature, max_tokens, output anonymization, 5 repetitions per arm (35 outputs per task), and blind evaluation by domain professionals.

The theoretical framework connects DA to sigma(t) optimization across output, identifies that the Artisan's Paradox has a discursive root (stereotypes vs archetypes in language production), and notes an isomorphism between FI and structured critical reading (the LER-DENSO protocol).

All results are from LLM-evaluated synthetic benchmarks [Hypothesis: no empirical validation by humans]. Human validation -- the critical next step -- has not yet been conducted. The investigation is promising but pre-validation; claims should be qualified as "preliminary synthetic benchmarks suggest..." rather than "we demonstrated."

## 12. Open Questions and Active Tensions

### The Artisan's Paradox

The most productive tension in the program: auto-generated frameworks consistently underperform human-crafted ones. In the FI benchmark, auto-generated FI on Sonnet produced -11.1% vs PE, while the conceptual claim is that FI should always be superior. In the DA investigation, human-crafted blueprints outperformed auto-generated ones by +122%. This creates a bootstrapping problem: if FI requires human expertise to craft, it cannot fully automate the reasoning transfer it promises. The resolution may lie in the Metacognitive mode (reduce the framework to evaluation criteria and self-audit rather than full scaffolding) and in developing a Framework Injection Wizard (a meta-agent guiding experts through framework creation rather than replacing them). The Artisan's Paradox has a discursive root: auto-generation produces stereotyped outputs while human craft produces archetypal ones.

### CE < PE: Cognitive Load or Measurement Artifact?

Context Engineering performed worse than Prompt Engineering in all benchmark scenarios, consistent with Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory (context without organizational framework equals extraneous load). But this finding needs qualification: the CE implementations in the benchmark may not represent CE at its best. The Protocol v2 addresses this by requiring SOTA versions of both PE and CE as comparators.

### Framework Composition and Interference

When multiple frameworks are injected simultaneously, do they compose (combine strengths) or interfere (conflict)? The SYMBIONT investigation raised a parallel question: do coordination mechanisms from different biological species compose? Preliminary evidence suggests they do when each addresses a distinct problem, but interference occurs when mechanisms address overlapping problems. This remains unvalidated for FI.

### Human Validation Gap

The most critical open question is methodological: all benchmark results are from LLM-evaluated synthetic benchmarks. No human professional evaluation has been conducted. The DA Protocol v2 designs a rigorous 7-arm study with blind evaluation by domain professionals, but execution requires recruiting evaluators and human-crafting blueprints and frameworks -- tasks the author must perform personally.

### The Model Capability Boundary

Both the Adaptive FI finding and the STEER isotropy finding suggest that the effectiveness of structured intervention depends on intrinsic model capability. As models improve, will FI-scaffold become universally harmful? Will all models eventually require only metacognitive frameworks? The answer has implications for the long-term relevance of the entire research program.

### Scaling and Reproducibility

STEER's results are across 6 models and 4 BEIR datasets -- solid but not exhaustive. IMI's 84 tests validate functionality but not large-scale performance. SYMBIONT's observations are preliminary from a prototype. Moving from "promising results on synthetic benchmarks" to "validated at scale on production workloads" remains the program's primary empirical challenge.

### FI Template Standardization

AGORA Intelligence identified the absence of a standard format/template for injectable frameworks as a critical silence (severity 5/5). The planned adaptation of Gagne's 9 Events of Instruction as an FI template schema addresses this, but the work has not been completed [Depth: 2 — Gagne cited via secondary].

## Conclusion: The Shape of the Program

The Artisanal Intelligence Program is, at its core, an attempt to answer one question: how should humans and AI systems communicate? The answer it proposes -- through the transfer of complete reasoning methods, grounded in 2,500 years of philosophical tradition and validated empirically -- is radical in its ambition and methodical in its execution.

The program's architecture reflects its author's cognitive style: everything connects to everything, each project is a node in a larger organism, and the naming of concepts is itself a foundational act. "Digital pidgin," "Framework Injection," "Semiotic Density Engineering," "Artisan's Paradox" -- these are not just labels but epistemic tools that constitute the realities they describe.

The strongest empirical findings are: FI improves smaller models by +9.3% [Validated: benchmark, 3 cases | Triangulation: 1], CE consistently underperforms PE [Validated: benchmark | Triangulation: 1] (validating Sweller), the Artisan's Paradox is real and measured at +122% in delivery architecture [Validated: DA investigation, 10 tests | Triangulation: 1], STEER achieves F1=0.91 on automatic operation routing [Validated: 30 experiments, 6 models, 5 BEIR datasets | Triangulation: 3] with isotropy as the hidden variable, and IMI achieves 100% recall on multi-hop causal chains versus RAG's 75% [Validated: benchmark | Triangulation: 1].

The most important open challenge is human validation: moving from LLM-evaluated synthetic benchmarks to blind evaluation by domain professionals. The Artisan's Paradox itself demands this -- if the program's central claim is that human expertise cannot be fully automated, then its own validation cannot rely solely on automated evaluation.

As a Lakatosian research program, the Artisanal Intelligence Program is theoretically progressive (generating novel predictions and concepts) and empirically emerging (building the validation infrastructure). Its hard core -- intelligence is craft, expertise is transferable, governance is constitutional -- remains unfalsified. Its protective belt -- specific taxonomies, benchmark numbers, implementation choices -- continues to evolve with each investigation. The positive heuristic points clearly forward: validate with humans, standardize framework templates, test framework composition, scale to production workloads, and build the Framework Injection Wizard that makes the approach accessible beyond its creator.
